Achaemenid Empire and the Greco-Persian Wars

This was the very first Persian Dynasty, founded by Cyrus the Great ~(550 BCE) and eventually conquered by Alexander III of Macedon ~(330 BCE). It was during this time that the famous Greco-Persian Wars happened.

Descriptions of ancient Persia may be biased, as our knowledge primarily comes from the Greek historian Herodotus, who considered non-Greek populations to be barbarians. The first video provides a non-biased and concise overview of ancient Persia and its first ruler, Cyrus the Great. The last video offers a well-done explanation of the third Persian invasion, which included the battles of Thermopylae and Salamis.

The Macedonian Empire and its successors

Alexander III of Macedon is considered one of the greatest commanders in human history, earning the prefix "Great". However, his empire didn't last long, and after his death, it was divided amogst the generals, known as the diadochi. This period saw the spread of Greek culture, known as Hellenism.
